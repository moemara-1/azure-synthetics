# Azure Synthetics Marketing Copy Research

## Sources reviewed

- FTC Health Products Compliance Guidance: health-related advertising must avoid misleading express or implied claims and needs adequate substantiation before publication.
- FDA RUO / IUO guidance and 21 CFR 809.10: RUO language should stay tied to laboratory research and avoid clinical diagnostic or therapeutic representations.
- Nielsen Norman Group web-writing research: users scan pages; concise, scannable, objective copy outperformed promotional copy.
- Baymard product-page UX research: product pages are where buyers decide; useful images, descriptions, specifications, buy-section details, and shipping/handling information reduce friction.
- CXL clarity guidance: copy should use customer language, visual hierarchy should put logo, value proposition, and CTA near the top, and button language should stay consistent.
- Competitor sweep: research-peptide sites commonly lead with COA/documentation access, third-party testing language, storage/shipping notes, proof status, support access, and RUO disclaimers.

## Copy rules used

- Lead with compound identity, vial amount, format, documentation status, and storage notes.
- Avoid "high-converting," "miracle," "clinical confidence," outcome promises, dosing, diagnosis, treatment, mitigation, cure, or human-use language.
- Use proof-status language instead of vague quality badges: "Documented now," "Available on request," or "Not publicly shown."
- Keep CTAs concrete: "Shop Catalog," "Review Standards," "Contact Support."
- Make support copy operational: documentation request, order reference, storage issue, repeat-buyer setup.
- Keep claims narrower where evidence is preclinical or mechanism-led.
