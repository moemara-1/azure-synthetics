# Azure Synthetics Storefront Implementation Brief

Date: 2026-04-23

## Strategic position
- Azure should feel like a premium, documentation-aware RUO storefront.
- The differentiator is not “hardest claims.” It is a cleaner blend of:
  evidence-tiered copy, premium presentation, operational clarity, and repeat-buyer trust cues.

## Messaging principles
- Lead with research seriousness, not consumer aspiration.
- Use premium language for:
  release discipline, cold-chain handling, documentation posture, buyer diligence, and catalog clarity.
- Use conservative language for:
  biology, outcomes, benefits, or anything that can drift into therapeutic claims.

## UX principles
- Every important page should answer:
  what is this, what proof posture exists, what is the handling burden, and what should the buyer do next.
- Remove fake or dead-end interactions.
- Increase variety in the image system:
  product hero renders, grouped vials, lab scenes, documentation visuals, and subtle brand textures.

## Content model decisions
- Product-level additions:
  compound alias, research summary, evidence tier, mechanism summary, documentation status, proof surface label, SEO keyphrase, and meta description.
- Category-level implementation:
  theme-managed collection profiles with SEO intro, trust framing, image, and FAQ content.
- Proof-status convention:
  documented now, available on request, not publicly shown.

## UI decisions
- Visual direction: clinical luxury.
- Typography: more refined and distinctive, with stronger hierarchy and better body readability.
- Layout: more editorial rhythm, fewer generic card moments, better archive and product structure.
- Motion: restrained reveal and interaction polish only.

## QA bar
- No unsupported therapeutic claims.
- No fake public COA or batch lookup claims.
- Strong mobile usability on navigation, accordions, collections, product purchase flow, and cart / checkout.
- Structured data and metadata should support SEO without introducing false statements.
