# Azure Synthetics Peptide Evidence Matrix

Date: 2026-04-23

## How to use this document
- This is an internal positioning and copy-governance document.
- Evidence tiers are for merchandising and claim boundaries, not medical advice.
- Public copy should stay strongest on sourcing, handling, documentation posture, batch discipline, and research framing.
- Public copy should stay conservative on biological outcomes unless a compound has clear current human evidence.

## Evidence tiers
- Tier A: Strong contemporary human clinical signal, usually randomized or registrational obesity/metabolic data.
- Tier B: Human signal exists, but the evidence base is smaller, older, narrower, or more indirect than Tier A.
- Tier C: Mostly preclinical or translational literature, with limited human data.
- Tier D: Emerging, fragmented, or market-led interest that outruns the literature.

## Current catalog dossiers

### BPC-157
- Aliases: Body Protection Compound 157, pentadecapeptide BPC-157.
- Commercial positioning: recovery / repair, musculoskeletal, gut-adjacent, athlete-heavy demand.
- Evidence tier: Tier C.
- Why: recent review literature describes broad preclinical support but very limited human data; a small 2025 pilot safety paper exists, but that does not justify therapeutic merchandising.
- Safe public angle:
  sourcing discipline, RUO positioning, investigational status, lot traceability, storage / handling, documentation availability.
- Forbidden copy:
  tendon healing, ligament repair, gut repair, post-surgery recovery, pain reduction, human-use instructions.
- Implementation note:
  this SKU should feel premium and evidence-aware, but every benefit sentence should be framed as research interest or literature context, not expected user outcome.

### MOTS-c
- Aliases: MOTS-c, mitochondrial-derived peptide MOTS-c.
- Commercial positioning: mitochondrial / longevity, metabolic signaling, performance-adjacent curiosity.
- Evidence tier: Tier C to low Tier B.
- Why: there is meaningful mechanistic and preclinical literature plus systematic-review/meta-analysis coverage of circulating MOTS-c associations, but the public market narrative still runs ahead of definitive human intervention evidence.
- Safe public angle:
  mitochondrial signaling research, metabolic homeostasis literature, investigational longevity category, research summary and evidence-tier labeling.
- Forbidden copy:
  fat loss, energy enhancement, longevity extension, insulin-sensitivity improvement as promised end-user result.
- Implementation note:
  strongest public merchandising surfaces should focus on “research category sophistication” rather than outcome promises.

### CJC-1295 / Ipamorelin
- Aliases: CJC-1295 DAC, Ipamorelin, GH secretagogue stack.
- Commercial positioning: GH secretagogue / protocol stack.
- Evidence tier: Tier B.
- Why: CJC-1295 has human randomized endocrine data showing prolonged GH/IGF-1 stimulation in healthy adults; ipamorelin has human pharmacokinetic / pharmacodynamic literature and older pharmacology literature. The evidence is real, but it is not a green light for broad consumer outcome claims.
- Safe public angle:
  protocol architecture, variable-kit merchandising, product format clarity, mechanism summary, stack-aware documentation, investigational endocrine signaling literature.
- Forbidden copy:
  muscle growth, anti-aging, sleep optimization, body recomposition, performance enhancement promises.
- Implementation note:
  this is the cleanest place to present “mechanism summary” and “evidence tier” as first-class product fields.

### Retatrutide
- Aliases: LY3437943, tri-agonist targeting GIP / GLP-1 / glucagon receptors. Current repo lineage: GLP-3.
- Commercial positioning: metabolic / incretin flagship.
- Evidence tier: Tier A.
- Why: strong contemporary human clinical signal from phase 2 obesity data, plus active phase 3 registrational program design published in 2025.
- Safe public angle:
  current obesity-research relevance, tri-agonist mechanism summary, refrigerated handling discipline, evidence-tier leadership, documentation posture, investigational research context.
- Forbidden copy:
  direct-to-consumer weight-loss promises, equivalence to approved therapies, dosing instructions, claims implying lawful retail human-drug substitution.
- Implementation note:
  this should read like the catalog’s sharpest flagship while still remaining RUO-first and FDA-aware.

## Broader market notes

### Incretin / metabolic leaders
- Retatrutide stands out for strongest forward-looking human evidence in the current repo-aligned catalog.
- Tirzepatide and semaglutide dominate commercial search intent, but public marketing in this category carries the highest current regulatory attention.
- Survodutide is worth watching as a benchmark for emerging obesity-research positioning.

### Recovery / repair demand pattern
- BPC-157 remains one of the most commercially visible peptides.
- Market demand is much stronger than the human evidence base.
- Copy must be built around investigational posture and documentation discipline, not outcome certainty.

### GH secretagogue demand pattern
- CJC-1295, Ipamorelin, and combo stacks remain staple catalog items across research-peptide stores.
- Buyers respond to stack clarity, variation logic, and dose-format transparency even when public claims stay conservative.

### Longevity / mitochondrial demand pattern
- MOTS-c is frequently merchandised using high-concept longevity language.
- Stronger sites increasingly need evidence-tier framing to avoid sounding speculative.

## Copy governance rules
- Use “research-use only,” “investigational,” “literature-aware,” “evidence-tiered,” “documentation-first,” and “handling-aware” language.
- Use “mechanism summary” only when the mechanism is framed as current literature context rather than promised effect.
- Do not use medical, therapeutic, or human-administration instructions.
- Do not imply FDA approval, prescription equivalence, or sameness to approved drugs.
- Do not imply the presence of public COAs, batch lookup, or stability dossiers unless the site actually exposes them.
